Genre - Thriller

Thriller

Thriller novels usually involve a protagonist who is working to fight a foreign power, criminal organization, or a ruthless villain. The conflicts often involve takes of espionage, financial crimes, politics, or some type of adventure. No matter what, thrillers have huge stakes. That’s what makes them thrilling. Often described as “heart-pounding” and “fast-paced”, thrillers are rife with apprehension, action, and violence. Whether humanity is threatened or billions of dollars are at stake, these high-intensity conflicts are essential to a thriller’s plot. The excitement of thrillers comes from the heightened emotional response within the reader as the protagonist races toward a goal. They are not about solving a mystery, but rather about the high tension throughout the story and the feeling of catharsis at the very end. Because of this, thrillers usually include high-intensity moments involving very fast vehicles, weapons, explosions, and death, and the pacing of the story is one of its most important components.

Types of Thriller Novels

Thrillers form a broad genre with multiple sub-genres, with novels often fitting into more than one at a time. Here are nine of its most popular categories.

  • Crime Thriller
  • Techno-Thriller
  • Medical Thriller
